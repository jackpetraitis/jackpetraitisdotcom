  • Completed: July 2014
  • Topics: Web UX, UI Design

Art of Intrusion

The Art of Intrusion is another one of Kevin Mitnick's novels that I enjoyed greatly. He interviews fellow hackers: white hats, black hats, and those somewhere in-between. The book is written as case studies and he uses quotes from the interviewees to put the reader in the moment. Then he uses his own knowledge plus anecdotal evidence to explain how the hacking took place and what security professionals can do to prevent it in the future.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1Bw11lu
  • Completed: Winter 2014
  • Topics: Hacking, Pentesting

Best Hash For Your Cash

In late 2013 I got heavily involved in the world of Scrypt Mining. By March of 2014 I had 8 miners with 16 video cards. As I was building my mining farm I decided I wanted to make sure I was getting the best "bang" for my buck when I was buying these cards. That is why I created www.besthashforthecash.com

The purpose of the site was to pull the latest cards listed on eBay, average their prices, and divide the average hashrate these cards got by the average price I could get from eBay. In the end this little website helped me make sure I wasn't overpaying for my cards and managed to get a few good deals using it.

  • Client: Scrypt Miners
  • Date: March 2014
  • Tech: AngularJS, C#, HTML, CSS, eBay APIs

Travel Times Tracker

The Travel Times idea spawned from a personal necessity. I was working in Schaumburg and living in the city. The variance for my travel times was very large. Some times it could take as little as 35 minutes. Other times it would take over 90 minutes! Obviously there were a lot of factors influencing these travel times, such as Hawks games, Bulls games, events, and good old fashioned rush hour. I decided to leverage Google Maps Directions API along with some basic information about city events for a given day and display it on a webpage for my own information.

  • Client: Myself
  • Date: January 2015
  • Stack: JavaScript + AngularJS, HTML, CSS
