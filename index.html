Don't Make Me Think

I picked up this book based off of some Amazon suggestions. This book was written over 10 years ago and recently revisited and totally updated. It does a great job explaining what internet users scan for on webpages and how to design with that information in mind.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1k5AWos
  • Completed: July 2014
  • Topics: Web UX, UI Design

Javascript: The Good Parts

I recommend anyone using Javascript in any capacity to pick up and read this book. It's a relatively short book, but it covers the best (and worst) parts about Javascript.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1rMU3pR
  • Completed: March 2014
  • Topics: Javascript, Programming, Best Practices

Ghost In The Wires

This was an exciting book I picked up to read over the summer. I ended up devouring it in a few days. The story of Kevin Mitnick inspired me to learn more about hacking and take a class in Computer Security.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1tnB1XE
  • Completed: Summer 2012
  • Topics: Hacking, Computer Celebrities

Art of Intrusion

The Art of Intrusion is another one of Kevin Mitnick's novels that I enjoyed greatly. He interviews fellow hackers: white hats, black hats, and those somewhere in-between. The book is written as case studies and he uses quotes from the interviewees to put the reader in the moment. Then he uses his own knowledge plus anecdotal evidence to explain how the hacking took place and what security professionals can do to prevent it in the future.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1Bw11lu
  • Completed: Winter 2014
  • Topics: Hacking, Pentesting

Lean In

A female co-worker of mine was reading this book and recommended it to me. We were talking about the scarcity of women within the Software Engineering field, and she enlightened me with some of the struggles she has experienced in the field. This book is a must read for anyone interested in helping women advance to a true 50/50 share and representation in the engineering workforce.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1dPntJa
  • Completed: February 2015
  • Topics: Women & Leadership

Startup Mixology

I attended a Tech Cocktail event in 2012 and got a kick out of entrepreneurs thinking I was a potential investor yet I was only a bearded developer still in school. Still, the experience sowed the seed for my interest in the startup community, and when I saw that this book was written by an Illinois alumnus I had to read it. I learned a lot about the startup process and was able to apply some of it to my failed attempt at joining a startup in 2014. All in all it was a great learning experience and I now have some starting points should I ever pursue my own entrepreneurial dreams.

  • Amazon: amzn.to/1ytXjEh

Sweet Potato Recipe Blog

My great aunt is really great. She loves sending me Sweet Potato Recipes. I used to HATE sweet potatoes. She's been doing it for about 5 years now. She'll send recipe clippings in the mail and fill my inbox with recipes she found on the internet. I love her, and I love sweet potatoes....now, that is. But now I have all these recipes... Why not throw them in a database and do something dumb with them? That's how this website was born.

  • Date: January 2015
  • Tech: EmberJS, HTML, CSS, NodeJS, MySQL

Thrillhouse Radio

Listen Now!

Thrillhouse Radio started as an ambitious project between 3 roommates. I had brought up the idea of starting a "house" radio station. "House" meaning a radio station hosted out of our home, but also meaning a station featuring House music. The idea expanded to include a "Brent Reads the Reader Radio Hour" along with an eventual option to have our audience build the radio's playlist. The station is still a work in progress, and is going up and down in accordance with that. But it's meant to be a fun project for my roommates and our friends. Sometimes I play the station in my car while I'm out ridesharing on Uber or Lyft.

  • Client: Please Tune In
  • Date: May 2015
  • Stack: AWS, Wordpress, Winamp, Ice Cast2
